Abstract

Traffic congestion is a critical urban challenge, causing delays, wasted fuel, and environmental harm. As Smart City initiatives expand, predictive analytics has become vital for sustainable mobility. This study compares three approaches—Linear Regression, Random Forest, and LSTM networks—on the Metro Interstate Traffic Volume dataset, which integrates weather, holiday, and temporal features.

Results show that LSTM consistently outperforms traditional and ensemble models, delivering higher accuracy and lower error values. These findings highlight the potential of deep learning for adaptive traffic control, real-time routing, and sustainable transport strategies.

Introduction

Urbanization and rising vehicle ownership have overwhelmed existing road networks, costing billions in lost productivity and increasing emissions. Predicting traffic patterns allows authorities to act proactively through adaptive signals, rerouting, and promoting public transit. With the advent of 5G networks and connected vehicles, predictive systems will be central to intelligent transportation.

Traditional traffic management strategies often struggle with sudden fluctuations caused by weather, holidays, or unexpected events. By combining historical patterns with real-time data, machine learning and deep learning models provide cities with the ability to anticipate congestion before it happens and respond more effectively.

Literature Review

Earlier models such as ARIMA and Kalman Filters work well for simple time-series, but fail under complex traffic patterns. Advances include:

  • Random Forest: Handles nonlinear relationships, reduces overfitting.
  • LSTM: Captures long-term dependencies in sequential data.

Recent studies confirm that LSTM models consistently outperform traditional and ensemble approaches in real-world traffic prediction tasks.
